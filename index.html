He is resuscitated with IV fluids and placed supine with leg elevation, with improvement in his symptoms and blood pressure normalizing to 124/82 mmHg. He is hemodynamically stable.

While admitted, which of the following medications should be AVOIDED in this patient?

Dobutamine should be AVOIDED

Dobutamine is a positive inotrope and chronotrope that increases contractility and heart rate. In HOCM, the LVOT obstruction is DYNAMIC — it worsens with increased contractility (stronger systolic anterior motion of the mitral valve), increased heart rate (less filling time), decreased preload (less blood to fill the LV), and decreased afterload (less resistance to outflow). Dobutamine increases BOTH contractility and heart rate, which would dramatically worsen the LVOT gradient and could precipitate hemodynamic collapse, ventricular arrhythmia, or sudden death.

Why the other options are incorrect:

  • A. Metoprolol (beta-blocker) is FIRST-LINE therapy for HOCM. It reduces heart rate (allows more diastolic filling), reduces contractility (decreases dynamic obstruction), and is used for symptom management. It is NOT avoided — it is desired.
  • B. Verapamil (non-dihydropyridine CCB) is SECOND-LINE therapy for HOCM when beta-blockers are insufficient or not tolerated. It reduces heart rate and has negative inotropic effects. It is beneficial, not harmful.
  • C. Phenylephrine (pure alpha-1 agonist) increases SVR/afterload WITHOUT increasing contractility or heart rate. In HOCM with hypotension, it is actually the preferred vasopressor because increased afterload reduces the LVOT gradient. It is safe and beneficial.
  • D. CORRECT — Dobutamine (positive inotrope/chronotrope) worsens dynamic LVOT obstruction and is CONTRAINDICATED in HOCM.
  • E. Disopyramide (class IA antiarrhythmic) is actually used therapeutically in HOCM because of its strong negative inotropic properties, which reduce the LVOT gradient. It is a treatment option, not something to avoid.
High-Yield Pearl: HOCM: AVOID anything that worsens obstruction — inotropes (dobutamine, milrinone, digoxin), vasodilators (nitrates, dihydropyridine CCBs, ACE inhibitors in large doses), dehydration, Valsalva. BENEFICIAL: beta-blockers, non-DHP CCBs (verapamil), disopyramide, phenylephrine (for acute hypotension), IV fluids. For hypotension in HOCM: IV fluids + phenylephrine. NEVER give inotropes or vasodilators.
